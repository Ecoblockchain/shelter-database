What it is for

Why

Shelter is one of the most costly and visible humanitarian interventions and at the same time a vital asset for affected people to rebuild their lives. Confronted with the reality of rising numbers of disasters where sheltering presents a key component of the humanitarian assistance, the Humanitarian Shelter Sector is challenged to upscale delivery of, cost-effective, sustainable and context specific shelter solutions.

What

Extensive experience in implementing emergency, transitional and permanent shelters exists within the humanitarian shelter sector. This knowledge needs to be documented and made available as reference and decision support to speed up the planning process in future responses. It can also support preparedness and long term impact assessments.

How

The IFRC-SRU has been tasked to build up an inventory of shelters documented in a systematic, methodological manner that particularly captures technical details, construction practices, climatic conditions, availability and suitability of materials and techniques and other information. The database is a shared knowledge base open for any agency to upload their shelter solution.
